## Study 1: Management Support and Employee Performance in Digital Workplace (Grzesiak & Ulrych, 2025)

**Full Citation:**
Grzesiak, M., & Ulrych, N. (2025). Management support and employee performance in digital workplace. *Central European Management Journal*, 33(1), 79-98. https://doi.org/10.1108/CEMJ-06-2023-0238

**Study Overview:**
- **Population:** Remote/hybrid workers in digital workplace contexts
- **Sample Size:** n = 1,000
- **Design:** Cross-sectional quantitative study 
- **Key Finding:** Management support strongly predicts performance (r = .649); partial mediation through digital workplace quality

---

### CAT MANAGER APPRAISAL QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

**Q1: This study is a ____**
- **Answer:** Cross-sectional study
- **Rationale:** Takes a "snapshot" of 1,000 workers at one point in time; examines relationships between management support, digital workplace, and performance simultaneously; no tracking of same workers over time


**Q2: Was the sample randomly selected?**
- **Answer:** unclear
- **Rationale:** Article does not specify random sampling method
- **Impact:** Possible selection bias (more engaged workers may volunteer); however, large sample size helps 

**Q3: Was the sample size large enough?**
- **Answer:** YES - Excellent
- **Rationale:** n = 1,000 provides >95% power to detect small-medium effects; far exceeds minimum requirements for SEM (need 10-20 per parameter; typically 200+ for SEM); much larger than typical management studies


**Q4: Is it unlikely that data dredging has taken place?**
- **Answer:** YES 
- **Rationale:** Clear theoretical framework stated upfront (management support → digital workplace → performance); theory-driven variable selection; reasonable number of paths tested; all significant at p < .01 (not borderline); peer-reviewed publication in established journal


**Q5: Were reliable and valid measurement methods used?**
- **Answer:** YES
- **Rationale:**
  * Management support: Likely multi-item scale (standard in organizational research; typical α > .80)
  * Digital workplace quality: Multi-dimensional construct with validated measures
  * Employee performance: Self-reported or supervisor-rated (common in field studies)
- **Assessment:** Standard organizational research measures; likely reliable but self-report bias possible


**Q6: How large was the effect size?**
- **Answer:** Large  
- **Effect Sizes:**
  * r = .649 (Management Support ↔ Performance) = LARGE effect per Cohen (>.50)
  * r = .657 (Digital Workplace ↔ Performance) = LARGE effect
  * r = .648 (Management Support ↔ Digital Workplace) = LARGE effect
  * β = 0.762 (MS → DW) = VERY LARGE path coefficient
  * β = 0.575 (DW → EP) = LARGE path coefficient
  * β = 0.275 (MS → EP direct) = SMALL-MEDIUM path coefficient
- **Practical Significance:** Strong relationships explain 42-43% of variance (r² = .42); these are robust, meaningful effects in organizational research

### OVERALL CAT MANAGER TRUSTWORTHINESS RATING

**Final Rating: 60% - TRUSTWORTHINESS**


**Major Limitations for Causation:**
- ❌ Cross-sectional design cannot establish temporal order (does management support cause performance, or does performance lead to more support?)
- ❌ No random assignment means cannot rule out confounding variables (e.g., organizational culture, industry effects)
- ❌ No control group means cannot isolate management support effects
- ❌ No confidence intervals reported (precision unknown)
- ❌ Possible common method bias (if same respondents rated all variables)

**What This Study CAN Do (Strengths):**
- ✅ Show management support and performance are STRONGLY ASSOCIATED/CORRELATED
- ✅ Demonstrate mediation pathway exists (support → digital workplace → performance)
- ✅ Large representative sample (n=1,000) increases generalizability
- ✅ Use structural equation modeling (tests complex relationships)
- ✅ Recent, relevant data (2025) in post-pandemic remote work context
- ✅ Very large effect sizes (r = .64-.66) suggest robust relationships

**What This Study CANNOT Do (Weaknesses):**
- ❌ Prove management support CAUSES performance improvement
- ❌ Rule out reverse causation (high performers → receive more support)
- ❌ Establish that X leads to Y over time (causation requires longitudinal design)
- ❌ Measure feedback frequency as specific mediator (uses digital workplace instead)

## Study 2: Performance Feedback, Goal Clarity, and Public Employees' Performance (Park & Choi, 2020)

**Full Citation:**
Park, S., & Choi, W. (2020). Performance feedback, goal clarity, and public employees' performance. *Sustainability*, 12(7), 3011. https://doi.org/10.3390/su12073011

**Study Overview:**
- **Population:** Public sector employees
- **Sample Size:** n = 2,630
- **Design:** Cross-sectional
- **Key Finding:** Performance feedback improves performance through goal clarity mediation (β = 0.022, p < .001); autonomy moderates the relationship

---

### CAT MANAGER APPRAISAL QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

**Q1: This study is a ____**
- **Answer:** Cross-sectional study
- **Rationale:** Collects data from 2,630 employees at single time point; examines relationships between feedback, goal clarity, and performance simultaneously; no longitudinal tracking


**Q2: Was the sample randomly selected?**
- **Answer:** Yes
- **Rationale:** Large public sector sample (n=2,630) suggests systematic sampling from employee database; public sector research often has better access to random samples than private sector


**Q3: Was the sample size large enough?**
- **Answer:** Yes
- **Rationale:** n = 2,630 is VERY large for SEM research; provides >99% power to detect even small effects (r = .05); far exceeds all minimum requirements; one of largest samples in performance feedback literature


**Q4: Is it unlikely that data dredging has taken place?**
- **Answer:** Yes
- **Rationale:** Clear theoretical model stated (feedback → clarity → performance); tests specific mediation hypothesis; includes pre-specified moderator (autonomy); all effects significant at p < .001 (highly conservative); published in reputable journal with peer review


**Q5: Were reliable and valid measurement methods used?**
- **Answer:** Yes
- **Rationale:**
  * Performance feedback: Multi-item validated scale (likely from established literature)
  * Goal clarity: Well-established construct in organizational psychology
  * Performance ratings: Objective supervisor ratings (reduces self-report bias)
  * Autonomy: Standard validated measure
- **Assessment:** Uses validated scales; objective performance measure (not self-report) is major strength


**Q6: How large was the effect size?**
- **Answer:** SMALL 
- **Effect Sizes:**
  * r = .165 (Feedback ↔ Goal Clarity) = Small-medium per Cohen (.10-.30)
  * r = .136 (Goal Clarity ↔ Performance) = Small-medium
  * r = .037 (Feedback ↔ Performance direct) = VERY SMALL (almost zero)
  * β = 0.165 (Feedback → Goal Clarity) = Small-medium path
  * β = 0.136 (Goal Clarity → Performance) = Small-medium path
  * β = 0.022 (Mediation effect) = SMALL but significant
  * R² = .184 (Model explains 18.4% of performance variance) = Small-medium
- **Practical Significance:** Effects are modest but meaningful; goal clarity explains meaningful portion of performance; direct feedback effect is WEAK (key insight: feedback needs to create clarity to work)
- **Context:** Public sector often shows smaller effects due to bureaucracy, unions, standardized roles

**Q7: How precise was the effect size?**
- **Answer:** YES 
- **Reported Confidence Interval:** 95% CI for indirect effect (mediation) = [0.015, 0.030]
  * *Translation: We can be 95% confident the true mediation effect is between 0.015 and 0.030*

---

### OVERALL CAT MANAGER TRUSTWORTHINESS RATING

**Final Rating: 60% - TRUSTWORTHINESS**

Stronger than Study 1 due to exceptional sample size and objective performance measures, but still limited by cross-sectional design:

**Major Limitations for Causation:**
- ❌ Cross-sectional design cannot establish temporal order (does feedback improve clarity, or do clear goals prompt more feedback?)
- ❌ No random assignment to feedback conditions (not experimental)
- ❌ No control group (all employees receive varying feedback)
- ⚠️ Public sector only (limits generalizability to private sector/remote work)
- ⚠️ Pre-pandemic data (2020) - early remote work context

**What This Study CAN Do (Strengths):**
- ✅ Show feedback and goal clarity are SIGNIFICANTLY ASSOCIATED with performance
- ✅ Demonstrate mediation pathway (feedback → clarity → performance) - KEY for X→M→Y model!
- ✅ EXCEPTIONAL sample size (n=2,630) increases confidence
- ✅ Use objective performance ratings (not self-report) - reduces bias
- ✅ Test moderation (autonomy enhances effects)
- ✅ Rigorous SEM analysis with multiple fit indices
- ✅ Validates goal clarity as mediator (conceptually similar to "employee clarity deficits")
- ✅ Reports confidence intervals for mediation effect [0.015, 0.030] - demonstrates precision

**What This Study CANNOT Do (Weaknesses):**
- ❌ Prove feedback CAUSES clarity or performance improvement
- ❌ Rule out reverse causation (high performers → seek more feedback/clarity)
- ❌ Measure feedback FREQUENCY specifically (your M variable)
- ❌ Generalize to remote/hybrid contexts (pre-pandemic study, likely office workers)
- ❌ Apply to private sector without caution

## Study 3: What Makes Telework Work? Evidence of Success Factors Across Two Decades (Gohoungodji et al., 2023)

**Full Citation:**
Gohoungodji, P., N'Dri, A. B., & Matos, A. L. B. (2023). What makes telework work? Evidence of success factors across two decades of empirical research: A systematic and critical review. *International Journal of Human Resource Management*, 34(3), 605-649. https://doi.org/10.1080/09585192.2022.2112259

**Study Overview:**
- **Population:** Telework/remote work contexts globally
- **Sample Size:** 83 empirical articles synthesized (2000-2021)
- **Design:** Systematic review with vote-counting analysis
- **Key Finding:** 7 dominant success factors identified; supervisor support, communication frequency, and trust consistently associated with telework success across 2 decades

---

### CAT MANAGER APPRAISAL QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

**Q1: This study is a ____**
- **Answer:** Systematic review 
- **Rationale:** Synthesizes 83 primary empirical studies; does not collect new data; uses systematic search and selection criteria; vote-counting analysis to identify patterns


**Q2: Was the literature search comprehensive?**
- **Answer:** no/unclear 


**Q3: Did most included studies use a control group or before and after measurement?
- **Answer:** No/unclear

**Q4: Is it unlikely that important, relevant studies were missed?
- **Answer:** Yes 

**Q5: Was the process to select studies clearly defined and reproducible?
- **Answer:** yes

**Q6: Was the process to extract data clearly defined and was the outcome presented in a table?
- **Answer:** yes 
- **Issue:** This is a qualitative systematic review using vote-counting, NOT a meta-analysis


**Q7: How precise was the effect size?
- **Answer:** No CI were reported 

---

### OVERALL CAT MANAGER TRUSTWORTHINESS RATING

**Final Rating: 80% - TRUSTWORTHINESS**


**Major Limitations:**
- ⚠️ Vote-counting approach less precise than meta-analysis (no aggregate effect size)
- ⚠️ Heterogeneity across 83 studies (different designs, measures, contexts)
- ⚠️ Potential publication bias (positive findings more likely published)
- ⚠️ Quality of review depends on quality of underlying 83 studies
- ❌ Does NOT directly test X→M→Y causal pathway
- ❌ Does NOT measure feedback frequency as specific mediator

**What This Study CAN Do (Strengths):**
- ✅ Establish ROBUST CONSENSUS on telework success factors across 21 years
- ✅ Identify supervisor support and communication as dominant factors (directly relevant to your X variable!)
- ✅ Demonstrate trust is critical (inconsistent management undermines trust)
- ✅ Provide breadth of evidence across multiple contexts/countries
- ✅ High-impact publication (111 citations) = trusted by field
- ✅ Transparent, systematic methodology
- ✅ Conceptual framework integrates person-environment theory

**What This Study CANNOT Do (Weaknesses):**
- ❌ Provide precise aggregate effect size (r = .XX)
- ❌ Test causal X→M→Y relationships (synthesizes correlational studies)
- ❌ Directly measure feedback frequency as mediator
- ❌ Control for study quality variations across 83 articles
- ❌ Account for unpublished null findings

## CRITICAL EVIDENCE GAPS

### The BIG GAP: M Variable Not Measured
**NONE of the three studies directly measure feedback frequency (1.8 vs 4-6 meetings/month) as the mediator**

What we have instead:
- Study 1: Digital workplace quality as mediator (related but different)
- Study 2: Goal clarity as mediator (conceptually related to employee clarity deficits)
- Study 3: Communication frequency identified as success factor (but not tested as mediator)

**Implication:** Cannot definitively say "low feedback frequency CAUSES clarity deficits"

### Other Gaps:
1. **No longitudinal/causal designs:** Cannot prove X → M → Y temporal sequence
2. **No experimental evidence:** Cannot isolate feedback frequency effects
3. **No specific metrics match:** Studies don't use your 1.8 vs 4-6 or 3.4 vs 4.2 benchmarks
4. **Limited private sector data:** Study 2 public sector only
5. **Pre-pandemic data:** Study 2 from 2020 (early pandemic)

## IMPLICATIONS FOR PRACTICE

### What the Scientific Evidence CLEARLY Supports:

**Problem Definition (STRONG SUPPORT):**
✅ Manager support/feedback consistency associated with performance (r = .04 to .66)  
✅ Supervisor support is critical success factor for telework (Study 3 consensus)  
✅ YOUR PROBLEM (inconsistent manager guidance) undermines 3 of 7 dominant telework success factors  
✅ Goal clarity mediates feedback→performance relationship (Study 2 validates M role)

**Solution Direction (MODERATE SUPPORT):**
✅ Structured management practices improve performance  
✅ Mediation pathways exist (support works THROUGH intermediaries)  
✅ Communication frequency and quality matter  
✅ Trust is essential (inconsistent guidance destroys trust)

### What the Scientific Evidence DOES NOT Support:

❌ **Specific feedback frequency targets** (1.8 vs 4-6 meetings/month not tested)  
❌ **Causal claims** (improving feedback WILL improve performance)  
❌ **Precise ROI estimates** (effect sizes vary r=.04 to .66)  
❌ **Implementation feasibility** (organizational constraints not studied)  
❌ **Long-term sustainability** (studies are snapshots, not multi-year tracking)

### Evidence Triangulation Required:

**Scientific evidence alone = 60-70% of decision basis**

**MUST be combined with:**
1. **Organizational Evidence:**
   - Your actual feedback frequencies (is it really 1.8/month?)
   - Your actual clarity scores (is it really 3.4?)
   - Manager time availability and constraints
   - Technology infrastructure
   - Budget for training/tools

2. **Practitioner Evidence:**
   - Manager interviews: Why is guidance inconsistent? Barriers? Solutions?
   - Best practices from high-performing managers
   - Implementation feasibility and timeline
   - Change management challenges

3. **Stakeholder Evidence:**
   - Employee preferences and concerns
   - Manager buy-in and readiness
   - Cultural fit
   - Risk assessment
